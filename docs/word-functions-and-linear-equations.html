Calculus and Linear Algebra

Lesson 1 Word: Functions and Linear Equations

1.1 Skills Check

Solve the following problems to refresh the skills you have learned previously.

  1. What does PEMDAS stand for? Use PEMDAS to simplify (4+6)*2 + 3.
  2. Simplify: (-3)^(-4).
  3. Simplify: \sqrt[3]{-8} \times \sqrt{16}.
  4. Write the inequality -2 ≤ x < 3 in interval notation.
  5. Given g(x) = x + \frac{1}{x}, find g(-1), g(2), and g(a+h).

1.2 New Skills Practice

Topics to discuss:

  • Draw the graphs and give the rules for: a horizontal line, a general linear equation, a parabola (quadratic), a cubic, the reciprocal function, and the absolute value function.
  • How to find the domain of a rational function or a function involving a square root.
  • The elimination method for solving a system.
  • The revenue, cost and profit functions, including fixed costs, variable costs, and demand.

Practice the techniques discussed in class and in the online videos by solving the following examples:

  1. Find the equation of the straight line through the points (3,4) and (5,2).
  2. Find the equation of the straight line through the point (3,4) and parallel to the line 2x - 3y = 4.
  3. If f(x) = -3x + 7, evaluate f(-3) and determine the domain of 1/f(x) using interval notation.

  1. Solve the system:
    \[ \begin{cases} 2x + y = 2 \\ -2x + y = 2 \end{cases} \]

  2. Solve the system:
    \[ \begin{cases} 2x - 3y = 2 \\ 6x - 9y = 3 \end{cases} \]

  1. (APPLIED) A piano manufacturer has a daily fixed cost of $1,200 and a marginal cost of $1,500 per piano. Find the cost function \(C(x)\) for manufacturing \(x\) pianos in one day. Answer the following:
    1. What is the cost of manufacturing 3 pianos?
    2. What is the cost of manufacturing the 3rd piano that day?
    3. What is the cost of manufacturing the 11th piano that day?
    4. Graph \(C\) as a function of \(x\).
  2. (APPLIED) Anthony Altino is mixing food for his young daughter and wants the meal to supply 1 gram of protein and 5 milligrams of iron. He mixes cereal (0.5 g protein and 1 mg iron per ounce) and fruit (0.2 g protein and 2 mg iron per ounce). What mixture will provide the desired nutrition?

1.3 Self-Assessment

Time yourself and try to solve the following within 20 minutes:

  1. Find the equation of the straight line through (3,1) and parallel to \(6x - 2y = 11\).
  2. Solve:
    \[ \begin{cases} \frac{-2x}{3} + \frac{y}{2} = \frac{-1}{6} \\ \frac{x}{4} - y = \frac{-3}{4} \end{cases} \]
  3. If the addition or subtraction of two linear equations results in \(0 = 3\), what does this say about their graphs?
  4. Determine the domain of \(f(x) = \sqrt{x-10}\). Express your answer in interval notation.
  5. In 2005, the Las Vegas monorail charged $3 per ride with an average ridership of 28,000 per day. After raising the fare to $5, ridership dropped to 19,000. Determine a linear function that relates ridership \(q\) to fare \(p\).
