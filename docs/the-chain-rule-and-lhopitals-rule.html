Lesson 10 The Chain Rule and L’Hopital’s Rule

Lesson 13

Skills Check

Solve the following problems to refresh the skills you have learned previously.

  1. If \(f(x) = 3x^{2}\) and \(g(x) = e^{x}\), find \(f(g(x))\).

  2. If \(f(x) = \sin(x)\) and \(g(x) = \cos(x)\), find \(f(g(x))\).

  3. If \(h(x) = \ln(x^{2})\), find two functions \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) satisfying \(f(g(x)) = h(x)\).

  4. If \(h(x) = (2x + 5)^{10}\), find two functions \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) satisfying \(f(g(x)) = h(x)\).

  5. Use dominant terms analysis to determine the limit: \[\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{6x^2 - 5x + 1}{3x^2 - 9}.\]

Lesson 13

New Skills Practice

Topics to discuss:

  • The formula for finding the derivative of a composition of functions.
  • The statement of L’Hopital’s Rule for indeterminate forms \(0/0\) and \(\infty / \infty\).

Practice the techniques discussed in class and in the online videos by solving the following examples.

  1. Differentiate:

    1. \(f(x) = (3x - 1)^{10}\)

    2. \(y = (1 - x)^{-1}\)

    3. \(v(x) = 3^{2x + 1} + e^{3x + 1}\)

  1. Differentiate:

    1. \(f(x) = (x^2 + 1)^5 \ln x\)

    2. \(f(x) = \frac{1}{(x + 1)^2}\)

    3. \(g(x) = \ln(x^2 + 3)\)

  1. Differentiate:

    1. \(h(x) = \ln \left(\frac{9x}{4x - 2}\right)\)

    2. \(h(x) = 2[(x + 1)(x^2 - 1)]^{-1/2}\)

    3. \(h(x) = 3^{x^2 - x}\)

  1. Verify L’Hopital’s Rule can be applied, and then use it to evaluate the limit. Sometimes you may have to apply the rule more than once.

    1. \[\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^2 - 2x + 1}{x^2 - x}\]

    2. \[\lim_{x \to -2} \frac{x^3 + 8}{x^2 + 3x + 2}\]

    3. \[\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{6x^2 - 5x + 1}{3x^2 - 9}\]

    4. \[\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x^2}{e^x}\]

  1. (Applied) The average price of a two-bedroom apartment in downtown New York City during the real estate boom from 1994 to 2004 can be approximated by

\[ p(t) = 0.33 e^{0.16 t} \text{ million dollars } \quad (0 \leq t \leq 10), \]

where \(t\) is time in years (\(t = 0\) represents 1994). What was the average price of a two-bedroom apartment in downtown New York City in 2003, and how fast was it increasing?

  1. Find the equation of the tangent line to \[y = \ln \sqrt{2x^2 + 1}\] at \(x=1\).

Lesson 13

Self-Assessment

Time yourself and try to solve the following questions within twenty minutes.

  1. Differentiate: \[r(x) = \left(\sqrt{x+1} + \sqrt{x}\right)^3\]

  2. Differentiate: \[r(x) = \left(e^{2x^2}\right)^3\]

  3. Determine the limit: \[\lim_{x \to -\infty} \frac{4x^3 + x^2 - 2x}{x^2 - 7}\]

  4. Determine the limit: \[\lim_{x \to \frac{1}{2}} \frac{\sin(2x - 1)}{2x - 1}\]

  5. The total spent on research and development by the federal government in the United States during 1995–2007 can be approximated by

\[ S(t) = 7.4 \ln t + 3 \text{ billion dollars} \quad (5 \leq t \leq 19) \]

where \(t\) is the year since 1990. What was the total spent in 2005 \((t = 15)\) and how fast was it increasing?
